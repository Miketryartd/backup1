STAN

In this song, Eminem corresponds with a crazed fan who becomes increasingly unhinged as the story pogresses. This is one of his most well-received singles and was listed at #15 on VH1's Top 100 Hip Hop Songs of All Time.
Eminem intended for this song to be a message to fans who had written him disturbing letters indicating that they had taken The Slim Shady LP's violent lyrics seriously. Another reason Em wrote this was to "make the critics who were saying things about [him] feel stupid." His aim was to disprove those who felt he lacked talent and relied on shock value.
This song has cemented itself in histoy, with "Stan" being entered into the Oxford Dictionary as an informal noun. It is generally considered to be a portmanteau of "stalker" and "fan," but Oxford defines the term as:
An overzealous or obsessive fan of a particular celebrity.
In a 2011 Shade 45 appearance, Em revealed that:
There was a verse where [Stan] got out of the water. He escaped and came to my house to kill me. Then I had to kill him first, [but] I missed him, and he was in the hospital for like three weeks. Then he was pissed off that I didn’t write him get-well cards, so he came to kill me again, and in the last verse finally I just blew his head off.
In 2013, Eminem released a follow-up in the form of "Bad Guy"



BMW

The song "BMW" by Because (Ft. Leslie (PHL)) is about enjoying a romantic night out in a car. The song talks about how the couple's getaway is just the two of them in a car and how they should make the best of it. The lyrics paint a vivid picture of how the couple is taking in the moment, appreciating each other's company, and indulging into passionate moments together. The chorus emphasizes the importance of this private and intimate setting, as it talks about how the car is the perfect four seater to provide the space and privacy they need to be together. The emotion and excitement is further conveyed by the use of the words "fuck, fuckin' sa'king BMW Sedan" - which implies that they are truly enjoying their time in the car. Ultimately, this song is a celebration of the beauty of making romantic memories in a car.

Eastside

Lyrically, “Eastside” is a song about a couple’s journey through life, with all its trials and tribulations. The song describes two lovers who come from different backgrounds and who are trying to navigate the challenges that life throws their way. The first verse sets the scene for the rest of the song, where the two characters are introduced. We learn about their backgrounds and how they first met. The lines “She used to meet me on the eastside/In the city where the sun don’t set” describe the location where their relationship started, and where they spent most of their time together. Throughout the lyrics, we see a recurring theme of separation and longing for each other. The chorus describes the desire to be close to each other again, “Back and forth like a wave/Let go of me like a wave.” It’s a metaphor for the constant ebb and flow of a relationship. The second verse explores the struggles that the two characters face in trying to stay together. It’s about the challenges of making a relationship work when two people have different goals and priorities. The lyrics “She said, where’d you wanna go?/How much you wanna risk?” show the differing priorities of the two characters. The bridge then changes the perspective of the song, and we see Benny Blanco addressing the listener directly. It’s like he’s giving advice on what he has learned from his own experiences. The lyrics “Don’t let me down/Strikin’ up on my own/Darn, you know that I wanna be there” are a message of hope and a reminder that even when things get tough, we can overcome them with a positive attitude.
